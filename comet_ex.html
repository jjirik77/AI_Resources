What is an "Agentic" Browser?

Traditional browsers help you find information. An "agentic" browser like Comet, powered by Perplexity AI, helps you act on it.

It's a browser with a built-in assistant that can understand your goals in plain English. It can read your open tabs, connect to your email or calendar (with permission), summarize content, and even take actions on your behalf, like drafting emails or organizing research.

Implications for Academia

Productivity Boost: For students and faculty, agentic browsers can dramatically cut down on "digital admin" â€” time spent organizing tabs, searching emails, and scheduling. This frees up more time for deep work like critical analysis, writing, and teaching.

Smarter Research: The ability to summarize and synthesize information across multiple sources (including tabs, PDFs, and videos) can accelerate the research and learning process.

New Ethical Questions: These tools also raise important questions. With special "Learn Modes" that can generate quizzes and flashcards, and even assist with online exercises, educators must consider the line between a helpful assistant and a tool that undermines the learning process.
